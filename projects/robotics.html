WRO 2018
8 June - 9 June, 2018

By far the most complex robot I ever designed.

It had a vertically moving arm with a color sensor, serving four different purposes: following lines, detecting colors in front of it, collecting and storing 2x2 cubes, and lifting up objects by their hoops.

It also had an inner storage between its wheels that could hold all three objects of one kind.

This year our robot betrayed us on stage: it was supposed to do what's on the left in the above video, but it couldn't lift its arms back into the resting position after it was done emptying the fridge. It kept holding onto the handles instead, getting stuck.

So, as a solution, I managed to redesign the arm during the break, making it detachable with enough force (right side of the video).

This trick made us win the competition.

Achieved a national 3rd place in the senior category.

This robot was very similar to the one we used in WRO 2018, but much, much simpler.

Its main tool was just a grabbing arm that could move up and down, and its 'belly' could hold 3 objects at once.

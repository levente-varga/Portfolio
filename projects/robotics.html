WRO 2018
8 June - 9 June, 2018

By far the most complex robot I ever designed.

It had a vertically moving arm with a color sensor, serving four different purposes: following lines, detecting colors in front of it, collecting and storing 2x2 cubes, and lifting up objects by their hoops.

It also had an inner storage between its wheels that could hold all three objects of one kind.

FLL 2017
2017

in robot competition

This year our robot betrayed us on stage: it was supposed to perform just like you can see on the left side of the above video, however, it could not lift its arms back into the resting position after it was done emptying the fridge. It kept holding onto the handles instead, getting stuck in the process.

Unable to fix the arm under such a short period of time, I instead decided to quickly redesign the robot in-between two runs, making the arm detachable by design, with just the right amount of force applied (right side of the video).

This trick made us win the robot competition.

national

Achieved national 3rd place in the senior category.

The core concept of this year's robot (vertical arm, hollow bottom section) was very similar to the one we used in WRO 2018.

Its main tool was just a grabbing arm that could move up and down, and its 'belly' could hold three objects at once.

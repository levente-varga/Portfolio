About

Bachelor's thesis project at Budapest University of Technology and Economics

At that time Unity did not have a default ray-traced refraction shader, so I developed my own

The thesis also included experimentation with other built-in unity shaders and rendering techniques

Developed in Unity with HLSL

Can be applied to any object

Adjustable refraction index and color

Sep 2021 - Dec 2021

Refraction

The shader works with complex shapes

Refractive objects are visible through other refractive objects, unlike with Unity's build-in shader

Objects outside the viewport are also rendered this way

This image demonstrates different refraction indices

Other thesis topics

A thorough comparison between Unity's built-in shaders of the High Definition Render Pipeline was also part of the thesis. This included both visual and performance comparisons in suitable test scenes.

Some of those works can be found below.

Global illumination

Comparison between turned off (left), screen-space (middle) and ray-traced (right) global illumination.

A directional light was used to light the scene enclosed between two walls.

The screen-space shader produced a strange blue artifact below the white ball, even though the scene did not contain any object of that color.

Subsurface scattering

Comparison between turned off (left), screen space (middle) and ray-traced (right) subsurface scattering.

The monkey head was lit from behind. The screen-space shader produced a surprisingly good result.
